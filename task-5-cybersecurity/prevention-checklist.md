1. Secure Your UPI Payments
Never enter your UPI PIN to receive money. Scammers often send "Request Money" links pretending they are sending you a refund or a prize. A PIN is only required for sending money, not receiving it.

2. Verify Before Scanning
Before scanning a QR code at a small shop or canteen, ensure the name displayed on your screen matches the shop’s name. Scammers sometimes paste their own QR stickers over the original ones.

3. Use Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)
Enable 2FA on your college email (G-Suite), WhatsApp, and Instagram. Even if someone steals your password, they won't be able to log in without the code from your phone.

4. Lock Your SIM Card
Set a SIM PIN in your phone settings. If your phone is stolen, the thief won't be able to put your SIM into another device to receive OTPs for your bank accounts or social media.

5. Be Wary of "Modded" APKs
Avoid downloading "Cracked" or "Modded" versions of apps (like Spotify Premium or Game Mods) from unofficial Telegram channels. These often contain hidden spyware that logs your keystrokes.

6. Browse Safely on Campus Wi-Fi
Avoid logging into your bank or sensitive accounts while using free public or hostel Wi-Fi. If you must, use a trusted VPN to encrypt your data.

7. Spot the "Urgent" Phishing Scams
Ignore messages on WhatsApp or SMS claiming your "Electricity will be cut" or your "Bank KYC is expired." These are always scams. Official banks never ask for details over a text link.

8. Check Your Digital Footprint
Avoid posting photos of your Hall Tickets, Aadhaar Card, or Internship Offer Letters on social media. These documents contain your Full Name, DOB, and ID numbers which can be used for identity theft.
