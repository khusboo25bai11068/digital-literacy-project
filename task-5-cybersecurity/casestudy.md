Case Study: The "QR Code" UPI Refund Scam
What the Crime Is
UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud involves tricking victims into authorizing unauthorized debits from their bank accounts. A common variation is the "Reverse QR Scam," where the attacker convinces the victim that scanning a QR code is necessary to receive money, when in reality, it is a request to pay.

How It Happens (Step-by-Step)
The Bait: A student posts an ad on a platform like OLX or Facebook Marketplace to sell an old textbook or a lab coat.

The Contact: The scammer messages the student immediately, acting as an "eager buyer," and agrees to the full price without bargaining to build excitement.

The Deception: The scammer claims they are paying from a "Business Account" and sends a QR code via WhatsApp, telling the student, "Scan this to receive the advance payment."

The Execution: Once the student scans the code in their UPI app (like GPay or PhonePe), a "Pay" screen appears. The scammer urges them to "Enter your PIN to confirm the credit."

The Theft: As soon as the PIN is entered, the money is instantly debited from the student’s account instead of being credited.

Who is Targeted
College students and young professionals are primary targets because they frequently use digital payments and are often active on second-hand selling platforms.

Consequences
Victims suffer immediate financial loss, which is often difficult to reverse because UPI transactions are instantaneous. Beyond the money, it leads to significant mental stress and a loss of trust in digital banking systems.
